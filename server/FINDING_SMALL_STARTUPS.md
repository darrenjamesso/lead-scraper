# Finding Small-Scale & Early-Stage Startups

## ğŸ¯ How to Find Small Startups

### Use These Filter Combinations

#### 1. **Pre-Seed Startups** (Smallest - Just Getting Started)
- **Funding Stage**: Pre-Seed
- **ICP**: Startup (1-49 employees)
- **Expected Results**:
  - Funding: $100K - $1M
  - Team: 2-10 people
  - Just getting off the ground
  - Founders-led companies

#### 2. **Seed Stage Startups** (Small but Validated)
- **Funding Stage**: Seed
- **ICP**: Startup (1-49 employees)
- **Expected Results**:
  - Funding: $500K - $5M
  - Team: 5-25 people
  - Early product-market fit
  - First real customers

#### 3. **Very Small Teams**
- **ICP**: Startup (1-49 employees)
- **Any Funding Stage** (but will skew smaller)
- **Expected Results**:
  - Companies with tiny teams
  - Bootstrapped or angel-funded
  - Lean operations

## ğŸ’¡ Tips for Finding Small Startups

### 1. Focus on Early Funding Stages
- **Pre-Seed**: The smallest and earliest
- **Seed**: Still small but more established
- Avoid Series A+ if you want truly small companies

### 2. Use the ICP Filter
- **Always select "Startup (1-49 employees)"**
- This filters out bigger companies
- Focuses on companies with small teams

### 3. Search Specific Niches
Small startups often focus on specific niches:
- "AI copilot for [specific industry]"
- "API for [specific use case]"
- "Platform for [specific market]"

### 4. Look for Emerging Markets
- CleanTech (environmental solutions)
- Web3/Crypto tools
- DevTools
- No-code platforms
- AI agents

## ğŸ“Š What Makes a Startup "Small"?

### Funding Amount
- **Very Small**: Under $1M (Pre-Seed, Angel)
- **Small**: $1M - $5M (Seed)
- **Medium**: $5M - $15M (Early Series A)
- **Large**: $15M+ (Series A+)

### Team Size
- **Very Small**: 2-10 employees
- **Small**: 10-25 employees
- **Medium**: 25-50 employees
- **Growing**: 50-100 employees

### Company Age
- **Very Early**: Less than 1 year old
- **Early**: 1-2 years old
- **Established**: 2-4 years old

## âš ï¸ Important Notes

### Why Small Startups Are Different

**Advantages**:
- More accessible founders
- Hungrier for customers
- More flexible and agile
- Better pricing for early customers
- Opportunity to grow with them

**Challenges**:
- Less proven product
- Smaller team = more risk
- May pivot or shut down
- Less funding runway
- Fewer resources

### Best Practices for Small Startup Leads

1. **Check if they're still active**
   - Visit their website
   - Check social media activity
   - Look for recent updates

2. **Research the founders**
   - LinkedIn profiles
   - Previous companies
   - Technical background

3. **Understand their stage**
   - Just launched?
   - Have customers yet?
   - How much runway?

4. **Tailor your approach**
   - Talk to founders directly
   - Mention their funding announcement
   - Offer early adopter terms
   - Build a relationship
